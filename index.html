Exploring the Global Terrorism in 1997-2017

Global Distribution of Total Terrorist Incidents



From 1997 to 2007, the top three Asian nations in terms of total terrorist attacks were Pakistan, Afghanistan and India. Other Asian countries, on the other hand, experienced fewer terrorist strikes.

Furthermore, terrorist attacks were primarily concentrated in Northern Africa.
Russia is the European country with the most terrorist strikes in the north.



According to the bar chart, the total number of terrorist attacks has shown an upward trend. The greatest number of occurrences happened in 2014.

Fixing the period in the interval of 2014 reveals that Pakistan, Afghanistan and India remain prominent although Iraq has the highest number of terrorist events.

Death & Injury Rate


Type Selection:


From 1997 to 2017, the number of injuries and deaths per 10,000 people increased along with the increase in the number of terrorist incidents.

However, the injury rate per 10,000 people was always higher than the death rate except in 2014.

Meanwhile, three peaks can be seen in 2001, 2007 and 2014, which respectively are 4.2% and 5.7% injury rate and 6.6% death rate.



Tips: Selecting the rate type to view the specific values of the injury rate and death rate from 1997 to 2017.

The Number of Times Different Attack Types Occurred




During these 20 years, no matter what year it was, the type of terrorist attacks was dominated by explosions.

Therefore, we made bold assumption that explosions contributed the majority of the injury and death rate.

In addition, the amount of terrorist incidents caused by armed assaults has steadily grown since 2008.



Tips: Using the small diagram to select the specified time range.

The Number of Times the Weapon Used in Terrorist Incidents


Explosive materials and firearms are used as the primary weapons of terrorist attacks.

However, biological materials, chemical materials and vehicles are rarely used.
Radioactive elements as weapons only appeared in 2000 and 2007.

It is interesting that fake weapons are even an option;

Combined with the above figure, explosive terrorist attacks and explosive materials have similar trends in the past 20 years.

So, we speculate that explosive terrorist attacks are directly caused by explosive materials, rather than indirectly using other things to cause explosions.
